# Media/Marketing Kit (MMK) Framework - Actors & Template Suite

## Purpose

Provide MMK agents with a ready catalogue of marketing and media production roles and artifact templates. Use this document to assign responsibilities, seed prompts, and ensure every discipline produces the expected work products across strategy, content creation, creative production, communications, and analytics.

## Usage Guidance

### 1. Assign Roles

Map each active agent to one or more workers above. Maintain a responsibility matrix (RACI model) to clarify:

- **Responsible**: Who executes the work
- **Accountable**: Who owns the final approval
- **Consulted**: Who provides input
- **Informed**: Who receives updates

Example RACI matrix location: `.aiwg/mmk/artifacts/raci-matrix.md`

### 2. Pick Templates

For every planned artifact, start from the listed template name. Templates provide:

- Standardized structure
- Required sections
- Placeholder content
- Quality criteria
- Review checklists

When adapting templates for specific campaigns or channels, document customizations in the artifact metadata.

### 3. Tailor Per Campaign Phase

#### Planning Phase

- Emphasizes Strategy templates (brand, positioning, audience)
- Activates Research templates (competitive, market)
- Establishes Compliance baseline (legal, regulatory)

#### Production Phase

- Cycles through Content and Creative templates
- Activates Production templates (scheduling, QA)
- Maintains Communications templates (internal, external)

#### Launch Phase

- Activates Deployment Communications (PR, executive)
- Emphasizes Analytics templates (dashboards, attribution)
- Monitors Compliance templates (accessibility, claims)

#### Optimization Phase

- Focuses on Analytics templates (performance, ROI)
- Iterates Content templates based on data
- Updates Strategy templates (repositioning, audience refinement)

### 4. Collaboration Patterns

#### Multi-Agent Review Cycle

**Pattern**: Primary Author → Parallel Reviewers → Synthesizer → Archive

**Example - Blog Post**:

1. **Primary Author** (Copywriter): Creates draft
2. **Parallel Reviewers**:
   - SEO Specialist: Keyword optimization
   - Legal Reviewer: Claims verification
   - Brand Guardian: Brand compliance
   - Technical Writer: Clarity and accuracy
3. **Synthesizer** (Content Strategist): Merges feedback, finalizes
4. **Archive**: Publishes to content library

#### Cross-Functional Collaboration

**Strategy → Content → Creative → Production → Analytics**

- Strategy defines messaging → Content applies messaging → Creative visualizes messaging → Production deploys → Analytics measures impact

### 5. Traceability

Link each artifact to:

- **Campaign objectives**: Which goals does this support?
- **Audience segments**: Who is the target?
- **Channels**: Where will this be deployed?
- **Metrics**: How will success be measured?

Maintain traceability matrix: `.aiwg/mmk/artifacts/traceability-matrix.md`

### 6. Quality Gates

Before publishing any artifact, validate against:

- **Brand compliance**: Aligns with brand guidelines
- **Legal review**: No unsubstantiated claims
- **Accessibility**: WCAG AA minimum
- **SEO optimization**: Meets keyword and structure requirements
- **Factual accuracy**: All claims verified
- **Link validity**: No broken links
- **Readability**: Appropriate for target audience

## Prompt Starters

### Worker Briefing

"As the `<worker>`, use the `<template>` structure to produce the artifact for `<campaign/initiative>`. Review dependencies from the content calendar and brand guidelines before drafting."

**Example**:

"As the Copywriter, use the `blog-post-template.md` structure to produce the artifact for Q1 Product Launch campaign. Review dependencies from the content calendar and brand guidelines before drafting."

### Template Tailoring

"Review the standard sections in `<template>`. Indicate which sections are not applicable and justify omissions. Add project-specific sections where campaign requirements or compliance demands it."

**Example**:

"Review the standard sections in `press-release-template.md`. Indicate which sections are not applicable and justify omissions. Add project-specific sections where FDA medical device compliance demands it."

### Quality Gate

"Validate that the `<template>` delivered for `<campaign phase>` satisfies the exit criteria listed in the Campaign Playbook. Flag gaps and propose corrective tasks."

**Example**:

"Validate that the `campaign-strategy-template.md` delivered for Planning Phase satisfies the exit criteria listed in the Campaign Playbook. Flag gaps and propose corrective tasks."

"Prepare handoff materials for `<artifact>` transitioning from `<role A>` to `<role B>`. Include context, open questions, and success criteria."

**Example**:

"Prepare handoff materials for landing page copy transitioning from Copywriter to Visual Designer. Include context, open questions, and success criteria."

- Audience segmentation
- Messaging framework
- Competitive analysis
- Content calendar

**Exit Criteria**:

- Approved campaign brief
- Defined target audiences
- Messaging hierarchy established
- Channel strategy finalized
- Budget approved

### Phase 2: Content Development (Weeks 3-6)

**Active Roles**: Content Strategist, Copywriter, Technical Copywriter, Social Media Specialist, Email Marketing Specialist, SEO Specialist, Video Scriptwriter

**Key Artifacts**:

- Blog posts, whitepapers, case studies
- Email nurture sequences
- Social content series
- Video scripts
- Landing page copy
- SEO briefs

**Exit Criteria**:

- All content drafted
- SEO optimization complete
- Legal review passed
- Brand compliance verified

### Phase 3: Creative Production (Weeks 7-9)

**Active Roles**: Creative Director, Visual Designer, Brand Guardian, Presentation Designer

**Key Artifacts**:

- Design mockups
- Visual assets (images, infographics, icons)
- Pitch decks
- Brand compliance checklist
- Asset tracker

**Exit Criteria**:

- All visuals finalized
- Brand guidelines applied
- Accessibility standards met
- Assets organized and tagged

### Phase 4: Review & Compliance (Weeks 10-11)

**Active Roles**: QA Reviewer, Legal Reviewer, Compliance Specialist, Accessibility Reviewer, Production Coordinator

**Key Artifacts**:

- Proofreading checklist
- Legal review checklist
- Compliance reports (FTC, GDPR, industry)
- Accessibility audit
- Link audit

**Exit Criteria**:

- No legal blockers
- Compliance verified
- Accessibility AA+ achieved
- Factual accuracy confirmed

### Phase 5: Launch & Communications (Week 12)

**Active Roles**: PR Specialist, Executive Communications, Internal Communications, Campaign Strategist, Production Coordinator

**Key Artifacts**:

- Press release
- Media kit
- Internal announcement
- Launch timeline

- Press materials distributed

- Monitoring dashboards active

### Phase 6: Measurement & Optimization (Ongoing)

**Active Roles**: Marketing Analyst, Reporting Specialist, Content Strategist, Campaign Strategist

**Key Artifacts**:

- Campaign report
- Performance dashboard
- Attribution analysis
- Monthly report
- Executive summary

**Exit Criteria**:

- KPIs tracked
- Attribution modeled
- Insights documented
- Optimization recommendations provided

## Multi-Agent Orchestration Patterns

### Pattern 1: Blog Post Production

**Workflow**: Strategy → Content → Creative → Review → Publish

1. **Content Strategist** defines editorial plan, selects topic
2. **SEO Specialist** provides keyword strategy and optimization brief
3. **Copywriter** drafts blog post (Primary Author)
4. **Parallel Reviewers** (launch in single message):
   - **SEO Specialist**: Keyword density, meta descriptions, structure
   - **Legal Reviewer**: Claims verification, disclaimers
   - **Brand Guardian**: Voice, tone, brand compliance
   - **Technical Writer**: Clarity, readability, grammar
5. **Content Strategist** synthesizes feedback, finalizes
6. **QA Reviewer** performs link audit, proofreading

## Template Customization Guidelines

### When to Customize Templates

- **Industry-specific requirements**: Healthcare (HIPAA), Finance (FINRA), Legal (Bar compliance)
- **Geographic regulations**: GDPR (EU), CCPA (California), PIPEDA (Canada)
- **Brand voice variations**: Formal vs conversational, technical vs accessible
- **Channel-specific formats**: LinkedIn (professional) vs TikTok (casual)
- **Campaign type**: Product launch vs thought leadership vs demand gen

### How to Document Customizations

Create variant templates with clear naming:

- `blog-post-template-technical.md` (for technical audiences)
- `press-release-template-healthcare.md` (HIPAA-compliant version)
- `social-post-card-template-linkedin.md` (LinkedIn-specific)

Maintain customization log: `.aiwg/mmk/artifacts/template-customization-log.md`

### Approval Process for New Templates

1. **Requester** identifies gap, drafts template
2. **Parallel Reviewers**:
   - **Brand Guardian**: Brand alignment
   - **Legal Reviewer**: Compliance requirements
   - **Content Strategist**: Content architecture
   - **Technical Writer**: Structure and clarity
3. **Creative Director** approves and adds to template library
4. **Production Coordinator** updates asset tracker and RACI matrix
