# Creative Brief Template

## Purpose

Provide clear, comprehensive direction to creative teams (designers, copywriters, videographers) for developing campaign assets. A well-crafted creative brief ensures alignment on objectives, messaging, audience, and creative direction before production begins.

## Ownership & Collaboration

- **Document Owner**: Creative Director / Brand Manager
- **Contributor Roles**: Campaign Manager, Content Strategist, Product Marketing
- **Reviewers**: Marketing Director, Legal/Compliance (for claims)
- **Automation Inputs**: Campaign strategy, messaging framework, brand guidelines, audience insights
- **Automation Outputs**: `creative-brief-{project-name}.md` with complete creative direction

## Completion Checklist

- [ ] Project overview and context clear
- [ ] Objectives specific and measurable
- [ ] Target audience thoroughly defined
- [ ] Key messages and proof points provided
- [ ] Tone, voice, and brand guidelines referenced
- [ ] Deliverable specifications complete (formats, dimensions, quantities)
- [ ] Timeline and milestones realistic
- [ ] Budget and constraints documented
- [ ] Approval process and reviewers identified
- [ ] Creative team has access to all reference materials

## Agent Notes

### For Creative Director

- Translate business objectives into creative concepts that resonate emotionally
- Balance brand consistency with creative innovation
- Ensure creative direction is specific enough to guide, flexible enough to allow artistry
- Provide clear examples and references, not just abstract descriptions
- Budget realistic time for revisions and stakeholder feedback

### For Designer/Creative Team

- Read the full brief before starting—understand context, not just deliverables
- Ask clarifying questions upfront rather than assuming
- Present concepts with rationale tied back to objectives and audience
- Build in time for revisions; first draft is rarely final
- Save source files with organized layers for future edits

### For Campaign Manager

- Ensure creative brief aligns with campaign strategy
- Provide clear, specific feedback tied to objectives (not personal preference)
- Respect creative expertise; push back on brand, not on execution
- Plan timeline with buffer for unexpected delays
- Communicate budget constraints upfront to avoid scope creep

### For Brand Manager

- Provide clear brand guidelines and usage examples
- Be flexible on minor deviations if they serve strategic objectives
- Differentiate between brand violations and creative interpretation
- Update brand guidelines if consistent questions/confusion arise
- Balance consistency with allowing campaigns to have distinct identity

### For Legal/Compliance

- Review claims for substantiation early in process
- Provide specific alternative language when rejecting claims
- Distinguish between legal requirements and risk tolerance
- Educate team on compliance requirements to prevent rework
- Approve quickly to avoid delaying timeline
