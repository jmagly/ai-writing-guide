## Purpose

This template provides a structured framework for selecting, prioritizing, and optimizing marketing channels based on audience behavior, business objectives, and channel performance. An effective channel strategy ensures efficient budget allocation and coordinated multi-channel campaigns.

## 1. Channel Selection Framework

### 1.1 Selection Criteria

**Channels should be selected based on**:
- [ ] Audience presence (where target audience spends time)
- [ ] Campaign objectives (awareness, engagement, conversion)
- [ ] Budget efficiency (CAC, ROAS, CPL)
- [ ] Competitive presence (channels competitors use/neglect)
- [ ] Resource availability (team expertise, time)
- [ ] Integration capability (attribution, tech stack compatibility)

### 1.2 Channel Categories

**Owned Channels** (you control):
- Website
- Blog
- Email list
- Mobile app
- Social media profiles (organic)

**Paid Channels** (you pay for reach):
- Paid search (SEM)
- Display advertising
- Social media advertising
- Influencer partnerships (paid)
- Sponsored content/native ads
- Podcast advertising
- Traditional media (TV, radio, print)

**Earned Channels** (organic reach):
- Organic search (SEO)
- PR/media coverage
- Social media (organic)
- Word-of-mouth/referrals
- User-generated content
- Community forums

## Agent Notes

### For Channel Specialist
- Prioritize channels based on audience presence AND performance data (not assumptions)
- Build integrated channel strategies (channels work together, not in silos)
- Reserve 10-20% budget for testing new channels and scaling winners
- Monitor cross-channel attribution to understand assist value, not just last-click

### For Campaign Strategist
- Map channels to RACE framework (different channels serve different funnel stages)
- Ensure message consistency across channels while adapting format to platform norms
- Coordinate campaign timing across channels for maximum impact
- Build channel-specific optimization playbooks based on historical learnings

### For Marketing Analyst
- Implement unified tracking (UTM standards, consistent conversion definitions)
- Build multi-touch attribution models to understand channel interactions
- Monitor efficiency metrics (CPA, ROAS) AND effectiveness metrics (incrementality)
- Identify and eliminate underperforming channels ruthlessly
