## Purpose

This template provides a structured approach to creating compelling customer success stories that demonstrate product value, build credibility, and support sales conversations. Case studies follow the classic Problem-Solution-Results format with quantifiable outcomes.

## Customer Interview

### Interview Details

**Interviewee(s)**:
- Name: `___________`
- Title: `___________`
- Role in Project: `___________`
- Email: `___________` (for approval/fact-check)

**Interview Date**: `YYYY-MM-DD`
**Interview Method**: `[Phone / Video / In-person / Email questionnaire]`
**Duration**: `[XX minutes]`

### Interview Questions

**Background & Context**:
1. What was your role in evaluating and implementing [product/service]?
2. What was happening in your business that led you to seek a solution?
3. What specific challenges or pain points were you facing?
4. How were you addressing these challenges before [product/service]?
5. What were the limitations or costs of your previous approach?

**Evaluation & Decision**:
6. What criteria did you use to evaluate potential solutions?
7. What alternatives did you consider? (competitors, build vs. buy, etc.)
8. What made [product/service] stand out from alternatives?
9. Who was involved in the decision? What were their concerns?
10. What was the "deciding factor" that led you to choose us?

**Implementation & Experience**:
11. What was the implementation process like?
12. How long did it take from purchase to go-live?
13. What challenges did you encounter during rollout? How were they addressed?
14. How did our team support you during implementation?
15. What surprised you (positively or negatively) about the process?

**Results & Impact**:
16. What specific, measurable results have you seen? (quantify with metrics)
17. How has [product/service] changed your day-to-day operations?
18. What has been the impact on your team/customers/business?
19. How long did it take to see results? When did you reach break-even/ROI?
20. What results were unexpected or exceeded expectations?

**Recommendation & Future**:
21. Would you recommend [product/service] to others? Why or why not?
22. What advice would you give to someone considering [product/service]?
23. How do you plan to expand or evolve your use of [product/service]?
24. Is there anything else you'd like to share about your experience?

## Review Checklist

### Content Quality

- [ ] **Accuracy**: All metrics and claims verified with customer
- [ ] **Specificity**: Quantified results (avoid vague claims like "significant improvement")
- [ ] **Authenticity**: Customer voice preserved in quotes
- [ ] **Storytelling**: Follows clear problem → solution → results narrative arc
- [ ] **Clarity**: Written in plain language (avoid jargon unless customer-approved)
- [ ] **Voice & Tone**: Aligns with BRD-002 (Voice & Tone Guide)
- [ ] **Grammar**: Passes grammar/spelling check

### Customer Approval

- [ ] **Fact-Check**: Customer reviewed and approved all facts, metrics, quotes
- [ ] **Legal Review**: Customer legal/compliance approved content
- [ ] **Branding**: Customer approved use of logo, name, and images
- [ ] **Contact Information**: Customer approved listing of names/titles
- [ ] **Anonymization**: Customer preferences for anonymization honored
- [ ] **Approval Signature**: Written approval secured (email or signed form)

### Sales Enablement

- [ ] **Relevance**: Addresses key objections or questions from target buyers
- [ ] **Metrics**: Includes quantified ROI or business outcomes
- [ ] **Comparability**: Customer profile matches target prospect profile
- [ ] **Differentiation**: Highlights unique value vs. competitors (if applicable)
- [ ] **CTA Alignment**: CTA drives appropriate next step for sales process

### Technical & Legal

- [ ] **Permissions**: Rights secured for all images, logos, quotes
- [ ] **Disclaimers**: "Results may vary" or similar disclaimer if required
- [ ] **Privacy**: No PII exposed without consent
- [ ] **Trademarks**: Customer and third-party trademarks used correctly
- [ ] **Competitive Claims**: No disparaging or unsubstantiated claims about competitors

### Distribution Ready

- [ ] **SEO**: Title, meta description, URL optimized (for web version)
- [ ] **UTM Tags**: CTA links tagged for tracking
- [ ] **PDF Formatted**: PDF version designed and formatted (if applicable)
- [ ] **Accessible**: WCAG 2.1 AA compliant (alt text, color contrast)
- [ ] **Mobile-Friendly**: Responsive design for web version

## Distribution Guidance

### Pre-Launch

**Customer Coordination**:
- [ ] Confirm publish date with customer
- [ ] Provide customer with final version for records
- [ ] Coordinate social media sharing (tag customer, provide assets)

**Internal Prep**:
- [ ] Add to website case study library
- [ ] Upload PDF to sales enablement platform (Seismic, Highspot, etc.)
- [ ] Notify sales team of new case study (email, Slack)
- [ ] Add to CRM as sales asset

**Marketing Prep**:
- [ ] Schedule blog post (if creating blog version)
- [ ] Create social media posts (3-5 variations)
- [ ] Draft email announcement (to customers, prospects, partners)
- [ ] Add to email signature rotation

### Launch

**Publishing**:
- [ ] Case study live on website: `[URL]`
- [ ] PDF available for download: `[URL]`
- [ ] Social posts go live (tag customer for amplification)
- [ ] Email announcement sent

**Customer Amplification**:
- [ ] Ask customer to share on their channels
- [ ] Provide pre-written social copy and graphics
- [ ] Coordinate press release (if newsworthy)

### Post-Launch

**Promotion** (30-60 days):
- [ ] Share on social 2-3 times (different angles/quotes)
- [ ] Include in weekly/monthly newsletter
- [ ] Pitch to industry media for coverage
- [ ] Add to sales presentations and pitch decks
- [ ] Use quotes in ad campaigns (with permission)

**Performance Tracking**:
- [ ] Monitor case study page views
- [ ] Track PDF downloads
- [ ] Track CTA clicks and conversions
- [ ] Survey sales team: "How often do you use this case study?"
- [ ] Note which deals close with this case study in play

**Refresh Schedule**:
- [ ] 6 months: Update metrics if customer has new results
- [ ] 12 months: Full review and refresh (or retire if outdated)

## Agent Notes

### For Content Strategist
- Identify case study candidates from customer success team
- Map case studies to buyer personas and sales stages
- Ensure portfolio of case studies covers diverse industries, use cases, company sizes
- Track case study performance and prioritize refreshes

### For Customer Success Manager
- Identify enthusiastic customers with strong results
- Brief customer on case study process and benefits
- Conduct interview or facilitate connection with content team
- Support fact-checking and approval process
- Coordinate customer amplification post-launch

### For Copywriter
- Focus on storytelling, not feature lists
- Use customer's own words in quotes (avoid corporate speak)
- Lead with results, not product features
- Write for the buyer, not the existing customer
- Make it scannable (subheads, bullets, pull quotes)
